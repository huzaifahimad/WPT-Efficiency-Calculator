About WPT Efficiency Calculator

A precision engineering tool for estimating wireless power transfer efficiency in resonant inductive coupling systems, built to support EV charging research and coil design optimization.

📄 Research Foundation

WPT Efficiency Calculator implements electromagnetic models derived from published research on wireless power transfer for electric vehicle fast charging systems. The calculator uses the Neumann formula for mutual inductance between coaxial circular coils and Wheeler's approximation for self-inductance computation.

The system efficiency model is based on coupled magnetic resonance theory, where efficiency is determined by the coupling coefficient and quality factors of the transmitter and receiver coils operating at resonance.

View Published Research Paper (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19549898)

🧮 Mathematical Models

The following core formulas power the calculator:

Self-Inductance (Wheeler):
L = μ₀ · N² · π · r / 2

Mutual Inductance (Neumann):
M = (μ₀ · π · N² · r⁴) / (2 · (r² + d²)^(3/2))

Coupling Coefficient:
k = M / √(L₁ · L₂) = r³ / (r² + d²)^(3/2)

Quality Factor:
Q = ωL / R = 2πfL / R

System Efficiency (maximum, at resonance):
η = k²·Q₁·Q₂ / (1 + √(1 + k²·Q₁·Q₂))²

Note: The model assumes identical coaxial coils with copper wire (ρ = 1.68×10⁻⁸ Ω·m) and does not account for skin effect, proximity effect, or core losses. For high-frequency applications, actual efficiency may differ.
